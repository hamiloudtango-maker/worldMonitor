A close ally of US President Donald Trump said on Saturday that Gulf leaders should send their own children to fight on the front lines in the
Israeli
-US war on
Iran
.
Speaking on his “War Room” podcast, former White House strategist Steve Bannon said Gulf elites should form the “first wave” of any ground assault.
"Maybe we can get a couple or three of those princes in uniform. Got any kids in special forces? Let's line up those royal families and see how big they're talking," Bannon said.
He widened his attack to include Washington’s broader allies, accusing them of exploiting the United States.
“The Israelis are playing games with us, the Arabs are playing games with us, the Europeans are playing games. And what are we doing? Sending troops over there. Fine, President Trump needs options and alternatives to negotiate the military operational victory,” he said.

“We're gonna go redo what Alexander the Great did 2,300 years ago,” he said.
“If we do that, I want Arabs at the front,” he added, suggesting regional states that opposed the war should bear the human cost.
He went further, naming specific targets and allies.
“The first wave at Kharg Island send the
UAE
. MBZ is probably the best ally we have over there, a country that punches way above its weight. Fine. If you're all in, let's go. Shut down the Dubai "pirate cove" Iranian money laundering in UAE now,” Bannon said.
His comments came a day after Trump, speaking at a Saudi-backed investment conference in Florida, mocked Saudi Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman (MBS).
“He [MBS] said, you know, it’s amazing … a year ago, you were a dead country. Now, you’re literally the hottest country anywhere in the world,” Trump said.
“He [MBS] didn’t think this was going to happen … he didn’t think he’d be kissing my ass … he thought it’d be just another American president that was a loser … but now he has to be nice to me,” he added.
